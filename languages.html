Vue, JS, and the Frontend

My introduction into the frontend frameworks was in Vue, and I found it a joy to use. Since then I have done some smaller scale projects in React, and I have done a couple things using Svelte. I do admit to enjoying Svelte and Vue quite a bit.



Clojure

Within a couple months of starting at Track49 I was introcuded to Clojure. After the initially shell shock about the number of parenthesis, I found myself enjoying writing clojure quite a bit. Writing code in Clojure is fun, and that makes everything else easier. Clojure is still one of my favorite languages, and it also helps that I have production code that just works in it.



Rust

I bought into the hype of Rust and decided to learn it. After a couple months of on and off learning, I finally had the determination to jump in and actually commit to learning it. I think that there is enough Rust evangelism out there that I don't need to add to it, but I will say that I have enjoyed learning it, and look forward to using it more.



Nim

Nim is not a language many people have heard of, and I think it's a shame. Nim is by far the easiest compiled and statically typed language to learn that I have found. It is quite elegant in its expressiveness, and just behaves like a programmer would expect. It is a lesser known language, but has deploying novel ideas and in new ways for longer than most know. It really makes programming fun and interesting.
